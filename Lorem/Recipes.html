Recipes

In some cultures, especially the Corean and the Japnese, the hogfish, in particular the inshore hogfish is considdered as food. On this page you will find a few recipes that contain hagfish, or it's slime. We are no experts on cooking with hagfish, but got these recipes from respectively the following sources: Cookbook: Hagfish (Wikibooks), ifood.tv, sjbuzz.net.

Korean Hagfish

"To prepare them, they are sliced down the middle to remove the digestive tract, then marinated in a sauce used for Korean barbecue. Traditionally, the raw fish are then placed on a heated plate at the center of the table, where they are cooked and served, using scissors to slice the hagfish up. The cooked fish are moved to the side of the dish, with lettuce and gochujang (spices), no amount of which can mask the animal's distinct taste. The head, containing the skull is left on the fish, and if a foreigner dines with Koreans, the honor will be offered to the foreigner."

In the Corean culture the slime of the hagfish is used as a substitute for egg or eggwhite. The hagfish are being held in a big container and then iritated (for example with a stick) and the slime that comes out of the hagfish is then harvested.

Kkomjangeo Bokkeum

"This is a Korean dish prepared by stir frying the ingredients. Inshore hagfish is stir-fried in sauce and served with alcohol. The fish is sometimes stir-fried in its spicy seasoning and some vegetables on a grill in an unusual manner. The fish is then placed over perilla leaf and eaten. It is often paired with a spicy and sweet sauce which imparts sweet and spicy texture to the fish."


Source picture: Wikipedia

Hagfish Quenelles (Serkonan Fish Dumplings)

Ingredients:
2 Hagfish, skinned, boned and de-wormed
Butter
Flour
3 Eggs
Salt
Cheese
Krust Stock (or any briny fish stock)
White Wine

Steps
Make a paste of the butter, flour and eggs. Add salt to tast. Mince and
pound the hagfish small and combine into egg paste.

Bringg the stock to a low boil. Add the agg and hagfish mixture to the stock
by large spoonfuls and simmer for 30 minutes.

Arrange the boiled quenelles in a baking dish. top with generous
amounts of cheese and butter, and add the wine on the bottom of the dish.
Bake until the cheese melts and the quenelles fragrant.

Serve at once with strong beer. Serves four.
